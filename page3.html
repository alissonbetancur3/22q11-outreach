About 22q11.2 Deletion Syndrome

22q11.2 Deletion Syndrome is a genetic condition caused by a small missing piece of chromosome 22. Symptoms vary, and it can affect multiple systems in the body. It is sometimes called DiGeorge Syndrome.

Symptoms

  • Heart defects
  • Learning or developmental challenges
  • Speech delays
  • Feeding or growth issues
  • Behavioral differences

Diagnosis

Diagnosis is usually made through genetic testing, often a blood test. It can be identified before birth or during early childhood depending on symptoms.

Treatment & Support

There is no single cure, but treatments help manage symptoms:

  • Heart surgeries if needed
  • Speech and developmental therapies
  • Educational and learning support
  • Regular medical checkups
  • Family and community support programs
